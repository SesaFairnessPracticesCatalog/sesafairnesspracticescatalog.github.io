Our Study

Machine learning is now, more than ever, pervasive in the daily life of billion of people and companies, which often make decisions on the basis of the information provided by machine learning-enabled systems. Despite such an increasing spread, the reliability, accountability, and credibility of machine learning solutions may be threatened by risks connected to low software fairness, i.e., the inappropriate treatment of sensitive features that may lead the machine learner to act unfairly with respect to minorities. In the recent past, the software engineering research community has been giving machine learning fairness an engineering connotation, proposing empirical studies, novel metrics, and ad-hoc fairness-aware approaches. In spite of these research advances, we still highlight a lack of knowledge and categorization of the practices that may be used to engineer software fairness within the lifecycle of machine learning solutions. This study propose a novel catalog of software engineering practices to handle fairness within each step of an MLOps development pipeline. The catalog is established through two complementary research methods. First, we conduct a systematic mapping study to extract, categorize, and map onto MLOps pipelines a set of 28 practices proposed in the literature. Second, we define a survey study involving three samples of ≈50 practitioners each with experience in machine learning engineering, in which we validate the practices by assessing their frequency, impact, and application effort in practice. We distil from the catalog a set of actionable items and implications for software engineering researchers and practitioners.

Research question and Research Method

We use the Goal Question Metric to formalize the research questions of our study. The ultimate goal of our research was to elicit and evaluate the most common practices to handle fairness in the context of the development of ML-Intensive systems. The perspective was of both researchers and practitioners, who are interested in understanding the practices that can be used to address fairness throughout the development lifecycle, along with their characteristics. More specifically, our study was driven by two main research questions. First, we aimed at eliciting a complete set of practices that can be used to treat fairness within an MLOps development scenario. In particular, we asked:

RQ1. What are the working practices to deal with fairness within an MLOps workflow?

Then, we wanted to assess the practitioner’s perspective on the identified practices. In this respect, we formulated the following research question:

RQ2. How do practitioners evaluate the practices according to their working experience?

To address our research questions, we proceeded as overviewed in the following Figure. Specifically, the main idea behind RQ1 was to formalize a comprehensive catalog of practices that summarized and systematized the common strategies and methods proposed in the literature. We addressed this research question by first conducting a systematic mapping study: this followed the guidelines by Peterson et al. and aimed at extracting a list of raw practices proposed to deal with Machine Learning fairness. Afterwards, the raw practices were further analyzed using iterative content analysis sessions: we followed the guidelines by White and Marsh to refine the set of practices and map them onto the MLOps lifecycle. As reported in Figure, the outcome of this research question was composed of a validated catalog of practices, namely the set of practices coming from the systematic mapping study which we validated in the content analysis sessions.

As for RQ2, we designed a survey study targeting experts of the domain, i.e., data scientists and Machine Learning engineers, to assess how good the refined practices would actually be in practice: the survey study followed the guidelines proposed by Kitchenham and Pfleeger, Andrews et al., and Wohlin et al. The outcome of this step was an evaluated catalog of practices, namely an amplified version of the catalog which contained information about the frequency, impact, and effort required of each practice, as perceived by practitioners.

An overview of MLOps Pipeline

MLOps stands for Machine Learning Operations. MLOps is a core function of Machine Learning engineering, focused on streamlining the process of taking machine learning models to production, and then maintaining and monitoring them. MLOps is a collaborative function, often comprising data scientists, devops engineers, and IT. ML Ops Official Guide

Machine Learning Fairness
